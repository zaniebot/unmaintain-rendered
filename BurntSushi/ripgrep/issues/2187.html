Comment by @BurntSushi on 2022-04-18 19:40

--smart-case is "smart" because it detects whether everything in your pattern is lowercase. Only then will it enable case insensitive search. Otherwise, it becomes a normal case sensitive search. Smart case helps when you want your search to be by default case insensitive unless there is something that isn't lowercase in your pattern. So, e.g., rg -S 'foo' behaves like rg -i 'foo' and rg -S 'Foo' behaves like rg 'Foo'. That's the "smartness."
