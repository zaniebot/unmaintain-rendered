Comment by @BurntSushi on 2025-09-03 17:59

https://github.com/BurntSushi/ripgrep/blob/master/FAQ.md#posix4ever

Moreover, -E in grep is not equivalent to -e in ripgrep. Ironically, -e in ripgrep is equivalent to... -e in grep. -E means something different in grep and isn't applicable to ripgrep. Additionally, -E in ripgrep is already used in ripgrep for something else (specifying an encoding).

Doubly moreover, there are loads of differences between grep and ripgrep. While the intersection between them is certainly bigger than the symmetric difference, ripgrep cannot reasonably be expected to be used as a tool with a grep compatible interface. Or, indeed, an interface compatible with any other tool. So even if I did what you asked here, it wouldn't be enough to accomplish the stated goal.

You should probably use a smarter AI.
