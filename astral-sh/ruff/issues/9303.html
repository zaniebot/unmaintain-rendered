ISC Rule suggestion: Consistent no leading whitespace for multiline concatenation

#9303 opened by @mroeschke on 2023-12-28 20:24
@mroeschke

As of ruff 0.1.6, ISC002 accepts both the following multi-line string concatenation

z = (
    "The quick brown fox jumps over the lazy "
    "dog."
)
a = (
    "The quick brown fox jumps over the lazy"
    " dog."
)

It would be nice to have an additional rule where for multi-line concatenation that the next line does not start with a white space for visual consistency (e.g. enforce the format of z). I think the previous line could always have a optional trailing white space.
