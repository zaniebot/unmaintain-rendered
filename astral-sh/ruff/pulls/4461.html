If you take a look at the logic in tryceratops, they do a couple nice things:

  1. They check if all handlers are useless. I think this is correct, since you can use a "useless" handler to omit exceptions from subsequent handlers.
  2. They check that the body is only a raise, and that the argument is either the same as the "cause" (except ... as e: raise e) or implicit (except ... as e: raise). I think we should check those too.

Might also make sense to take a look at their test suite to ensure compatibility.
